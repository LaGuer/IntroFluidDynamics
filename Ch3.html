Chapter 3 Ideal Fluids

We’ll start our exploration of solutions to the Navier-Stokes equation with ideal fluids, which we’ll define as

  1. 1.

    having no viscosity, and

  2. 2.

    having constant density (incompressible).

Of course, real fluids always have some viscosity; however, as discussed in Section 1.6, in some cases an ideal fluid can be a good approximation to a real one. Keep in mind, however, that the boundary layer present in viscous fluids can never be described by an ideal fluid, so we’ll miss some of the physics due to that.

Under these assumptions, the Navier-Stokes equation is usually called Euler’s equation,

D⁢𝐮D⁢t=-1ρ∇p+𝐠. (3.1)

The incompressibility condition,

∇⋅𝐮=0, (3.2)

remains the same.
